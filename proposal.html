üéØ Objective

Pacemakers are class III, life-sustaining medical devices that require constant monitoring. Traditional systems rely on check-ins that may miss early failure signs. Our project proposes using a Digital Twin (DT) system‚Äîa virtual replica of the pacemaker‚Äîintegrated with AI and IoT to monitor in real-time, predict failures, and send automated alerts. Unlike existing systems, our DT includes AI-based prediction and individualized learning for each patient. Our goal is to shift from reactive to predictive maintenance, saving lives and reducing hospital dependency.

Existing literature supports DT applications in other sectors. Our project builds on these by focusing specifically on heart pacemakers and proposing full AI-IoT-DT integration with predictive features.

üõ† Proposed Design Approach

Our design includes IoT sensors to collect data like heart rate, voltage, and battery level. This is transmitted wirelessly to a cloud-based DT for real-time analysis. The DT uses manufacturer specs, patient history, and machine learning to compare expected vs actual performance. üìå Realistic Constraints

The project faces technical, legal, economic, and ethical constraints:

  • Regulatory: Must comply with ISO 14708-1, ISO 13485, HIPAA
  • Cybersecurity: Prevent unauthorized access & tampering
  • Manufacturability: Integrate without affecting pacemaker battery/lifespan
  • Cost: AI systems ($50K‚Äì500K), IoT sensors ($50‚Äì1M+)
  • Environment: e-waste from disposable sensors, electromagnetic interference
  • Ethics: AI transparency, accessibility for all income levels, trust in automation

Despite challenges, DT tech could reduce emergency visits, increase device lifespan, and make healthcare proactive and more cost-efficient.
