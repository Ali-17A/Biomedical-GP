üéØ Objective

Pacemakers are a Class III medical device that are life-sustaining. They require regular check-ups, but traditional monitoring is based on periodical check-ins, which may miss early signs of failure like battery draining or lead fractures. Our idea integrates a Digital Twin (DT)‚Äîa virtual real-time replica of the pacemaker‚Äîcapable of simulation, real-time monitoring, and predictive analytics.

This system aims to combine IoT sensors, AI-driven failure prediction, and automated alerts to enhance patient safety. Unlike some remote monitoring systems or existing digital twin studies, our design introduces full integration between the AI and IoT to produce proactive feedback and warnings.

üõ† Proposed Design Approach

Our design includes the following:

  • IoT Sensors: Collect real-time pacemaker data (e.g., heart rate, voltage, battery life).
  • Wireless Transmission: Sends data to the cloud via Wi-Fi or Bluetooth.
  • Digital Twin Simulation: Simulates pacemaker behavior based on:
    • Manufacturer specifications
    • Patient-specific calibration and pattern learning
    • AI-based continuous updates for anomaly detection
  • AI Anomaly Detection: Flags deviations and categorizes risks (low vs critical) to notify healthcare providers.
