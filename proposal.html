📄 Project Proposal

📘 Designing Workflow Models for Medical Device Failure Reporting – Digital Twin

🎯 Objective

Pacemakers are Class III life-sustaining medical devices. Traditional check-ups can miss early signs of failure... [keep your full text here]

🛠 Proposed Design Approach

  • IoT Sensors: Collect heart rate, voltage, and battery life.
  • Wireless Transmission: Sent via Wi-Fi/Bluetooth to DT cloud.
  • Digital Twin System: Simulates behavior using:
    • Manufacturer specifications
    • Patient-specific patterns
    • AI learning from past data
  • AI Anomaly Detection: Categorizes risks and sends alerts to experts.
  • System Diagrams (in progress):
    • Data Flow from Pacemaker to DT
    • AI Failure Detection Logic
    • Alert System Workflow

📊 System Comparison

Old SystemProposed System
Regular checkupsReal-time data
Failure after malfunctionPredictive AI alerts
Delayed interventionImmediate alerts

⚙️ Realistic Constraints

  • 📄 ISO 14708-1, ISO 13485, HIPAA compliance
  • 🔐 Cybersecurity: Encrypted transmission, anti-hacking
  • 🔋 Sensor efficiency: No major battery drain
  • 💸 Cost: AI $50k–$500k, IoT $50k–$1M+
  • 🌍 Environmental impact: e-waste and safety checks
  • ⚖️ Ethical: Patient data privacy, AI transparency
