üéØ Objective

Pacemakers are a Class III life-sustaining medical device. Traditional monitoring methods rely on periodic check-ups that may fail to detect early signs of issues like battery depletion or lead fractures. Our project introduces Digital Twin (DT) technology to provide real-time monitoring, predictive diagnostics, and immediate alerts. DT integrates IoT sensors, cloud computing, and AI to create a dynamic digital model of the pacemaker, continuously comparing real data to expected behavior. The result is a safer, more responsive patient care system with proactive failure detection.

üõ† Proposed Design Approach

The system uses IoT sensors embedded in pacemakers to collect vital data‚Äîheart rate, voltage, battery status. This data is transmitted wirelessly (Wi-Fi/Bluetooth) to a cloud-hosted Digital Twin. The DT replicates real-time device behavior and compares data against manufacturer standards, personalized patient norms, and AI-learned patterns. An AI engine evaluates deviations, classifying them into risk levels. Critical anomalies trigger alerts to medical professionals. Flowcharts demonstrate:
