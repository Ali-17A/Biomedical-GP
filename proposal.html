Objective

Pacemakers are Class III life-sustaining medical devices. Traditional check-ups can miss early signs of failure like battery drainage or lead fracture. Our project proposes integrating Digital Twin (DT) technology for real-time monitoring, predictive failure detection, and instant alerts.

A Digital Twin is a virtual replica of a physical system. Combined with IoT sensors and AI, it enables continuous data monitoring, anomaly detection, and automated alerts, transforming patient safety and medical responsiveness.

Proposed Design Approach

  • IoT Sensors: Collect data like heart rate, voltage, and battery life.
  • Wireless Transmission: Data sent via Wi-Fi/Bluetooth to a cloud-based DT.
  • Digital Twin System: Simulates pacemaker behavior using:
    • Manufacturer specifications
    • Patient-specific patterns
    • AI learning from past data
  • AI Anomaly Detection: Categorizes risks and sends alerts to experts instantly.
  • System Diagrams (in progress):
    • Data Flow from Pacemaker to DT
    • AI Failure Detection Logic
    • Alert System Workflow

Realistic Constraints

  • Compliance: ISO 14708-1, ISO 13485, HIPAA
  • Cybersecurity: Encrypted transmission, hacking resilience
  • Interoperability: Integrate with various pacemaker brands
  • Power Usage: Energy-efficient sensors
  • Cost: Development range: $50K – $1M+
  • Ethics: Data privacy, AI bias, fairness
  • Environmental: Device disposal, radiation effects
