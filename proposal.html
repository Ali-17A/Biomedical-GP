Project Proposal

1. Objective

Pacemakers are Class III medical devices that require regular check-ups, but traditional monitoring may miss early symptoms of failure. Our project proposes integrating a Digital Twin (DT) with pacemakers for real-time simulation and performance monitoring. DTs have been used in healthcare, aerospace, smart cities, and more.

By using IoT sensors, AI-based anomaly detection, and automated failure reporting, the DT system can alert healthcare providers of problems before they become life-threatening. This innovation offers continuous monitoring, personalized calibration, and improved patient safety.

2. Proposed Design Approach

The design includes IoT sensors in the pacemaker for real-time data on heart rate, voltage, and battery life. Data is sent via Bluetooth or Wi-Fi to a cloud-based Digital Twin. The DT uses:

  • Manufacturer specifications
  • Patient-specific calibration
  • AI-based adaptive learning from past data

AI detects anomalies, categorizes their severity, and sends alerts accordingly. Visual tools like system diagrams and flowcharts will explain:

  • Data flow from pacemaker to DT
  • AI-based failure detection steps
  • Alert system workflow

3. Realistic Constraints

Constraints include compliance with standards (ISO 14708-1, ISO 13485, HIPAA), high cybersecurity, interoperability with existing pacemakers, and minimal power impact. The system must be user-friendly and affordable. AI development and IoT integration may cost from $50K to $1M+.

Environmental impact (e-waste, interference), ethical concerns (data privacy, AI bias), and fairness (accessibility) must be addressed. With careful implementation, DTs can reduce healthcare costs, hospitalizations, and improve predictive maintenance.
